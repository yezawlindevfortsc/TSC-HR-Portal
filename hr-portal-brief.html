One page for management: what it is, what it costs, how we'll measure success, and the decision we're asking for now.

01 / WHAT & WHY

The proposal in four lines

An internal, HR-only web portal where our six-person HR department does its work with AI assistance — continuous compliance monitoring (DRC, permits, quotas, FCF, PDPA retention), AI-drafted documents on our letterhead, CV screening, and eventually payroll under strict human control.

Analysis of the department's full job scopes shows ~75% of HR tasks can be AI-led or AI-assisted — the value concentrates in compliance early-warning (avoiding MOM/BCA breaches) and removing manual data work. Humans keep every consequential decision.

04 / ADOPTION

Pilot & rollout plan

  1. Pilot (4 weeks): Executive (FW) + HR Manager use the Compliance Monitor and Documents modules alongside current methods. FW is chosen because permit/DRC alerts deliver the fastest visible value.
  2. Weekly feedback loop: 30-minute session; fixes shipped weekly. The pilot users become the internal champions.
  3. Department rollout: remaining four roles onboarded with half-day hands-on training; job aids kept inside the portal's Knowledge Assistant.
  4. Go/no-go for Phase 2: after 8–10 weeks of live use, review metrics against §03 and decide the payroll phase.
Build vs buy, honestly: off-the-shelf HR/ATS products exist and are cheaper for generic recruitment or leave tracking. Nothing off-the-shelf covers our Singapore-contractor compliance core — DRC/quota tracking, levy & waiver, BCA licence conditions, FCF enforcement, HRIQ coexistence. Custom is justified for that core; if scope ever drifts toward generic HR features that products already do well, we should buy, not build.
05 / RISKS

Top risks & how the design contains them

  • Payroll/statutory errors → deterministic code (never AI), maker-checker approval, and a parallel-run against the current process before anything is authoritative.
  • PDPA / data protection → HR-only access with MFA, Singapore-resident hosting, AI never sees raw personal data (de-identification gateway), full audit trail, independent security review before go-live.
  • Wrong regulatory rules encoded → all statutory figures verified by the named compliance owner (Ye Zaw Lin) before being hard-coded; rules kept configurable.
  • Key-person / maintenance risk → mainstream stack (Python/React), documented, tested; a named maintenance owner is a condition of go-live.
  • Adoption failure → pilot-first rollout with champions, not a big-bang switch.
